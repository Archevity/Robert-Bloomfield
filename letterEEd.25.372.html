Letter 372 - The Letters of Robert Bloomfield

372. Robert Bloomfield to Joseph Weston, undated fragment * 

The consequences arising from the application are too well known to be repeated; yet there are circumstances attending the progress of the poem through the press which are not known, and might be known with honour to all the parties concern'd. The first letter I received from Mr Lofft was to me a cordial, not to be described,...

last letter to George—May happen to get hold of it [1] 

Notes

* Tipped into a copy of Remains, BL, Bloomfield 164 BACK

[1] This sentence is written on the verso in another hand. BACK
